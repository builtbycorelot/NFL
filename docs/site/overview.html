NodeForm Language Overview

NodeForm Language (NFL) is a minimal graph notation for declaring computation and policy together. Nodes and edges build a self-describing graph that can execute across runtimes while tracking provenance and risk.

Why NFL and Why Now?

  • Software and regulations keep colliding. A shared semantic layer lets both evolve together.
  • Graphs express relationships better than ad-hoc APIs.
  • Hardware trust anchors such as PLight.token allow verified execution of graph logic.
